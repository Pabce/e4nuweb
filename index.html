About

The Electrons for
Neutrinos Collaboration

The extraction of mixing parameters in accelerator-based neutrino oscillation measurements relies on detailed understanding of neutrino-nucleus interactions and the reconstruction of incident neutrino energy. With improved detection technologies and neutrino production beams, nuclear interaction uncertainties are becoming a leading and limiting systematic for the analysis of neutrino oscillation measurements.

Building on the large similarity of electron- and neutrino-nucleus interactions, the electrons-for-neutrinos collaboration is leading a set of precision electron-nucleus interaction measurements at various beam energies and target nuclei to test, constrain, and validate models of neutrino-nucleus interactions.

We welcome all collaborators to join our e4nu effort.

Electron-Nucleus Interaction

A stylized view of an (e,e'p) process: the electron exchanges a virtual photon with the nucleus, is deflected, and a proton can be emitted from the excited nuclear state.

Exchanged boson: γ*
1.Electron approaches nucleus
2.Virtual photon γ* exchange
3.Electron deflects, nucleus changes state
4.Proton emission from the nucleus

Vector-vs-axial: the exchanged γ* is a vector boson, and electron scattering constrains the vector-current half of the neutrino-nucleus interaction puzzle. The weak axial response is the missing term that must be inferred through neutrino data and modeling.

Precision Measurements

Electron-nucleus interaction measurements at various beam energies and target nuclei using CLAS and CLAS12 detectors at Jefferson Lab.

Model Validation

Testing and constraining theoretical models crucial for neutrino-nucleus interaction modeling, including the GENIE event generator.

International Collaboration

A global effort spanning institutions across Israel, the United States, Spain, and the United Kingdom working together on neutrino physics.

Research Output

Publications

01

S. Dytman, M. Betancourt, N. Steinberg, L.B. Weinstein, A. Ashkenazi et al.

“Proton Transparency and Neutrino Physics: New Methods and Modeling”

arXiv: 2508.01905 [hep-ex]

New data-driven measurement of proton transparency using CLAS electron–nucleus scattering on He, C, and Fe targets. Transparency is defined as the ratio of events with a detected proton to quasi-elastic events where a proton should have been knocked out—avoiding model dependence of earlier extractions. Results are consistent with previous measurements but reveal that GENIE needs improved nuclear ground-state and FSI modeling, particularly at low proton momenta.

02

J. Tena Vidal, A. Ashkenazi, L.B. Weinstein, P.G. Blunden, S. Dytman, N. Steinberg

“A universal implementation of radiative effects in neutrino event generators”

Comput. Phys. Commun. 310 (2025) 109509 · arXiv: 2409.05736

Implements QED radiative corrections—real photon emission by incident and scattered electrons, plus virtual-photon exchange and vacuum polarization—into neutrino event generators using the extended peaking approximation. Validated within GENIE, the framework enables proper unfolding of radiative effects when benchmarking electron–nucleus data against generator predictions, closing a long-standing gap in e–A/ν–A comparisons.

03

A. M. Ankowski et al.

“Electron scattering and neutrino physics”

J. Phys. G: Nucl. Part. Phys. 50 120501 (2023) · arXiv: 2203.06853

Comprehensive topical review on how electron–nucleus scattering data constrain neutrino interaction models across the QE, resonance, DIS, and MEC channels from tens of MeV to several GeV. Outlines the critical role of e–A measurements in reducing nuclear-interaction systematics for DUNE, Hyper-K, and the broader oscillation program. A community white-paper with 53 authors spanning the e–A and ν–A fields.

04

M. Khachatryan, A. Papadopoulou, A. Ashkenazi et al.

“Electron-beam energy reconstruction for neutrino oscillation measurements”

Nature 599, 565–570 (2021) · doi:10.1038/s41586-021-04046-5

Landmark e4ν result exploiting the known electron beam energy as a proxy for the unknown neutrino energy to benchmark energy-reconstruction methods on CLAS data (C, Fe targets). Demonstrates that calorimetric reconstruction outperforms QE-based approaches, reducing biases from nuclear effects. Reveals that current neutrino generators mis-reconstruct the incident energy at the few-percent level—directly relevant for oscillation δCP measurements.

05

A. Papadopoulou, A. Ashkenazi et al.

“Inclusive Electron Scattering And The GENIE Neutrino Event Generator”

Phys. Rev. D 103, 113003 (2021) · arXiv: 2009.07228

First systematic test of GENIE’s electron-scattering mode against inclusive (e,e′) cross-section data. Incorporates radiative corrections and the SuSAv2-based QE+MEC model into the generator. Shows adequate agreement at lower beam energies but exposes discrepancies in the RES/DIS regime at higher energies, charting the path for improved nuclear modeling in neutrino generators.
